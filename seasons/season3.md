# Season 3 Overview

Season 3 explores the aftermath of Buffy’s departure from Sunnydale and focuses heavily on themes of identity, responsibility, and the consequences of choices.  
It introduces **Faith**, the rogue Slayer whose arc becomes one of the most important in the Buffyverse, and **The Mayor**, a charismatic yet sinister villain preparing for Ascension.

Key arcs:
- Buffy’s return home and complicated dynamic with Joyce and the Scoobies  
- Introduction and fall of Faith, the second active Slayer  
- The Mayor’s transformation into the Big Bad of the season  
- Angel struggling with guilt, purpose, and the consequences of Season 2  
- Graduation and the final showdown with The Mayor  

Season 3 is often considered one of the strongest and most cohesive seasons in the series.

## Episode List

|------|--------|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **S03E01** | *Anne* | **IMPORTANT** | Buffy starts a new life in LA, but is drawn into a demon-run labor dimension. | Shows Buffy’s emotional recovery after killing Angel; establishes themes of “identity” and introduces **Lily/Chanterelle**, who later reappears in *Angel* (the series). |
| **S03E02** | *Dead Man’s Party* | **DISPENSABLE** | A Nigerian mask raises zombies; tensions erupt as Buffy returns home. | Develops Buffy’s strained relationship with Joyce and the Scoobies; emotional foundation for the group’s resilience but no long-term plot points. |
| **S03E3** | *Faith, Hope & Trick* | **FONDAMENTAL** | Faith arrives in Sunnydale; new vampires Mr. Trick introduced. | **First appearance of Faith**, whose arc becomes central in Seasons 3, 4, and 7. Introduces **Mr. Trick**, a recurring villain; hints at the dangers of multiple Slayers coexisting. |
| **S03E04** | *Beauty and the Beasts* | **DISPENSABLE** | A monster-of-the-week with themes of abusive behavior; Angel mysteriously returns. | Angel’s return from Hell sets up his redemption arc; this episode confirms his feral state and that he remembers Hell, shaping his worldview in *Angel* (the spin-off). |
| **S03E05** | *Homecoming* | **DISPENSABLE** | Buffy and Cordelia battle assassins during the Homecoming dance. | First seeds of the **Slayer vs. Slayer rivalry** between Buffy and Faith; introduces *The Slayerfest* tournament concept. |
| **S03E06** | *Band Candy* | **IMPORTANT** | Adults regress to teenage behavior due to cursed candy. | Reveals more of Giles’ rebellious past and deepens his bond with Joyce; establishes **Mayor–Ethan Rayne connections**, relevant to the season’s conspiracy arc. |
| **S03E07** | *Revelations* | **IMPORTANT** | A Watcher arrives to evaluate Faith, exposing her insecurities; Angel’s secret training is revealed. | First major cracks in **Faith’s loyalty**; establishes her distrust of authority. Introduces Gwendolyn Post and hints at corruption or dishonesty within the Watchers’ Council. |
| **S03E08** | *Lovers Walk* | **FONDAMENTAL** | Spike returns and unintentionally triggers several emotional breakups. | Spike’s philosophy speech foreshadows future Buffy/Spike arcs. Willow and Xander’s affair is exposed, significantly altering relationships. **Cordelia and Oz trajectories** shift because of this episode. |
| **S03E09** | *The Wish* | **IMPORTANT** | Cordelia’s wish creates an alternate universe ruled by The Master. | First appearance of **Anya** (as Anyanka), who becomes a main character from Season 4 onward. Establishes the concept of vengeance demons and alternate timelines, important lore for the Buffyverse. |
| **S03E10** | *Amends* | **IMPORTANT** | The First Evil manipulates Angel into suicidal despair. | First explicit appearance of **The First Evil**, who becomes the Big Bad of Season 7. Angel’s attempt to kill himself and miraculous survival deepen mythology around higher powers influencing events. |
| **S03E11** | *Gingerbread* | **DISPENSABLE** | A witch-hunting hysteria grips Sunnydale’s parents. | Shows the vulnerability of Sunnydale adults to supernatural manipulation; lightly foreshadows community paranoia themes in later seasons. |
| **S03E12** | *Helpless* | **FONDAMENTAL** | On her 18th birthday, Buffy loses her powers due to a Watchers’ Council test. | Exposes the Watchers’ Council’s harsh traditions; strains Buffy and Giles’ relationship until his firing. Sets up Buffy’s increasing independence and mistrust of Council authority. |
| **S03E13** | *The Zeppo* | **DISPENSABLE** | Xander has his own adventure while the others avert an apocalypse off-screen. | Shows Xander’s emerging maturity and role as the “heart” of the group, a theme pivotal to Season 4 and the finale of Season 7. |
| **S03E14** | *Bad Girls* | **FONDAMENTAL** | Buffy bonds with Faith over reckless slaying; Faith kills a human. | **Faith kills Deputy Mayor Finch**, the turning point that pushes her toward the dark side. Introduces the Mayor/Faith alliance through implication. |
| **S03E15** | *Consequences* | **FONDAMENTAL** | Faith spirals after killing Finch; the Watchers attempt retrieval. | Faith officially aligns against the Scoobies and moves toward the Mayor. Buffy rejects Council authority, continuing the independence arc. |
| **S03E16** | *Doppelgängland* | **IMPORTANT** | Alternate-universe vampire Willow appears in Sunnydale. | Expands Willow’s character possibilities; reinforces the “dark Willow” idea revisited in Season 6. Also shows Anya’s diminished powers and desire to regain full demon status. |
| **S03E17** | *Enemies* | **IMPORTANT** | Faith tricks Buffy and Angel; the Mayor manipulates both Slayers. | Confirms **Angel’s loyalty** and consolidates Faith’s role as the Mayor’s assassin. Exposes new details on the Mayor’s immortality preparations. |
| **S03E18** | *Earshot* | **DISPENSABLE** | Buffy gains telepathy after demon infection. | Telepathy ability reappears briefly in Season 4; Jonathan’s crisis sets up his later involvement in Seasons 4 and 6. |
| **S03E19** | *Choices* | **IMPORTANT** | Buffy captures the Box of Gavrok; Faith escalates the conflict. | The **Box of Gavrok** is crucial for the Mayor’s Ascension. Buffy asserts independence by rejecting Council constraints—key to her future leadership role. |
| **S03E20** | *The Prom* | **IMPORTANT** | Buffy ensures a monster-free prom; emotional closure for her and Angel. | Angel ends the relationship to give Buffy a future; establishes emotional groundwork for the spin-off *Angel*. The Class Protector award symbolizes Buffy’s value to Sunnydale. |
| **S03E21** | *Graduation Day (Part 1)* | **FONDAMENTAL** | Buffy confronts Faith; the Mayor’s plan nears completion. | **Buffy vs. Faith showdown** leaves Faith comatose, affecting Seasons 4 & 7. Angel drinking Buffy’s blood becomes vital lore for vampiric healing. |
| **S03E22** | *Graduation Day (Part 2)* | **FONDAMENTAL** | The Ascension begins; Buffy and the students fight the Mayor. | The Mayor becomes a demon and is destroyed. Faith’s coma, Angel’s departure for LA, and the collapse of Sunnydale High launch multiple major arcs (including *Angel* S01). |
