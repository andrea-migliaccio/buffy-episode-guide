# Season 7 Overview

Season 7 is the final chapter of *Buffy the Vampire Slayer*.  
The story returns to the show’s mythological roots, exploring the origin of the Slayer line and introducing the ultimate enemy: **The First Evil**, the primordial source of all evil.

Key arcs:
- Buffy steps into true leadership, becoming a general to the new Potential Slayers  
- Spike’s redemption arc after gaining his soul  
- The First Evil’s manipulations across the Hellmouth  
- The activation and training of the Potential Slayers  
- Willow’s recovery from Season 6 and eventual empowerment  
- The Scooby Gang’s final battle to destroy the Hellmouth once and for all  
- Setup for future developments in *Angel*, especially with Spike  

Season 7 focuses heavily on mentorship, legacy, and reformation of the Slayer myth.

## Episode List

| Code | Title | Importance | Description | Key Story Notes |
|------|--------|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **S07E01** | *Lessons* | **IMPORTANT** | Buffy trains Dawn while strange manifestations appear at the rebuilt high school. | The **First Evil** appears disguised as past villains (Warren, Glory, Adam, The Master, etc.). Sunnydale High is rebuilt directly on the Hellmouth, reestablishing it as the central battleground. |
| **S07E02** | *Beneath You* | **IMPORTANT** | A burrowing demon stalks Sunnydale; Spike struggles now that he has a soul. | Spike reveals that he **regained his soul** voluntarily. His breakdown in the church signals the beginning of his redemption arc. |
| **S07E03** | *Same Time, Same Place* | **DISPENSABLE** | A skin-eating demon targets Willow after her return from England. | Highlights lingering distrust toward Willow after Season 6, but does not advance major plotlines. |
| **S07E04** | *Help* | **DISPENSABLE** | Buffy, now a school counselor, tries to save a girl whose death seems inevitable. | Develops Buffy’s mentorship role, a theme that becomes central as Potentials arrive. |
| **S07E05** | *Selfless* | **IMPORTANT** | Anya confronts the consequences of a vengeance demon massacre. | Full deep-dive into **Anya’s backstory**: Aud, her transformation into a vengeance demon, and her break from D’Hoffryn. Significant for her end-of-season arc. |
| **S07E06** | *Him* | **DISPENSABLE** | A magical letterman jacket causes obsessive attraction. | Mostly comedic; no long-term relevance. |
| **S07E07** | *Conversations with Dead People* | **FONDAMENTAL** | The gang encounters manifestations of the dead, manipulated by the First Evil. | Reveals several key things: The **First Evil’s psychological warfare**, Joyce’s mysterious warnings to Dawn, and the loneliness/fragility of Buffy’s role. Major tone-setter for the season. |
| **S07E08** | *Sleeper* | **IMPORTANT** | Buffy investigates Spike after murders suggest he’s killing again. | Spike’s triggered programming (linked to the First) is revealed. First hints that the First can manipulate souled Spike through implanted commands. |
| **S07E09** | *Never Leave Me* | **IMPORTANT** | The First manipulates Spike; the Bringers attack. | The Bringers kidnap Spike to begin awakening the Turok-Han. Establishes the First’s military structure: Bringers, Ubervamps, and Caleb (introduced later). |
| **S07E10** | *Bring on the Night* | **FONDAMENTAL** | Buffy confronts the first Turok-Han while Potentials arrive in Sunnydale. | Introduction of the **Potential Slayers**. First appearance of the **Turok-Han**, an ancient proto-vampire, raising the season’s stakes. |
| **S07E11** | *Showtime* | **FONDAMENTAL** | Buffy fights the Turok-Han to restore hope among the Potentials. | Buffy demonstrates hope and strategy; Spike’s torture shows his value to the First. This episode cements Buffy as a true leader to the Potentials. |
| **S07E12** | *Potential* | **DISPENSABLE** | Dawn believes she might be a Potential Slayer. | Strengthens Dawn’s character arc and her place in the group, but no major plot elements originate here. |
| **S07E13** | *The Killer in Me* | **DISPENSABLE** | Willow transforms into Warren due to grief and guilt. | Deepens Willow’s trauma from Season 6, but has little direct impact on the main arc. |
| **S07E14** | *First Date* | **DISPENSABLE** | Buffy goes on a date while the gang learns more about the First. | Establishes that Principal Wood has a hidden past — his connection to the Slayer line and his mother, Nikki Wood. |
| **S07E15** | *Get It Done* | **IMPORTANT** | Buffy seeks power from the Shadow Men; Spike regains confidence. | Origins of the Slayer are revealed; Buffy rejects the patriarchal offer from the Shadow Men, symbolizing her future choice in the finale. Spike’s regained fighting strength is highlighted. |
| **S07E16** | *Storyteller* | **DISPENSABLE** | Andrew documents the gang for a video “diary.” | Andrew’s shift from villain to “redeemable” ally begins here. |
| **S07E17** | *Lies My Parents Told Me* | **IMPORTANT** | Buffy, Giles, and Wood clash over Spike’s role. | Reveals **Spike’s full siring backstory**, especially his relationship with his mother. Solidifies Giles–Buffy distance and Wood’s vendetta. |
| **S07E18** | *Dirty Girls* | **FONDAMENTAL** | Caleb arrives and slaughters several Potentials. | Introduction of **Caleb**, the First’s chief lieutenant. The Potentials suffer major losses. Raises the season to its final level of danger. |
| **S07E19** | *Empty Places* | **DISPENSABLE** | The gang loses confidence in Buffy’s leadership. | Prepares the emotional separation before the final two episodes. |
| **S07E20** | *Touched* | **IMPORTANT** | Buffy regroups separately while the Potentials and Scoobies find resolve. | Spike’s speech restores Buffy’s confidence — emotionally pivotal for both characters. |
| **S07E21** | *End of Days* | **FONDAMENTAL** | Buffy retrieves the Scythe and prepares for the final battle. | Introduction of the **Scythe**, a critical Slayer artifact. Sets up the empowerment spell. Buffy’s confrontation with Caleb builds to the finale. |
| **S07E22** | *Chosen* | **FONDAMENTAL** | Buffy leads the final battle against the First; the Hellmouth is destroyed. | Willow activates **all Potential Slayers** with the Scythe’s power. Spike sacrifices himself with the amulet. Sunnydale is destroyed, ending the Hellmouth once and for all. Sets up Spike’s return in *Angel* Season 5. |
